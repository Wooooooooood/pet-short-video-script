## Shot Type Selection Guide

Choose shot types based on the emotional goal of each moment:

| Goal | Recommended Shot | Why It Works |
|------|-----------------|--------------|
| Make audience laugh | Medium shot + slightly low angle | Captures full body comedy + pet's perspective |
| Create "aww" reaction | Close-up on face/eyes | Maximizes cute factor, shows expression detail |
| Build tension/suspense | Peeking through doorway/frame | Limited view creates curiosity about what's next |
| Show environment | Wide establishing shot | Sets the scene, shows pet's relationship to space |
| Emotional connection | Medium close-up at eye level | Equalizes pet and viewer, creates intimacy |
| Comedy timing | Static camera, pet enters/exits frame | Classic physical comedy technique |
| Show small size | Low angle with oversized props | Emphasizes cuteness of tiny pet |
| Show power/pride | Low angle looking up at pet | Makes pet look heroic/majestic (funny contrast for small pets) |
| Food/eating content | Top-down overhead | Clean view of food + pet's eating action |
| Sleepy/cozy content | Close-up with shallow depth of field | Dreamy, intimate, peaceful atmosphere |

## Camera Movement Selection

Match camera movement to the energy of the scene:

### Low Energy (Calm, Sleepy, Tender)
- **Static / locked off** - Let the pet's subtle movement be the only motion
- **Slow dolly in** - Gradually increase intimacy
- **Slow pan** - Gently reveal the scene
- **Subtle breathing motion** - Tiny float as if handheld by a calm person

### Medium Energy (Daily life, Playing, Exploring)
- **Gentle handheld** - Natural, TikTok-native feel
- **Tracking at walking pace** - Follow pet through environment
- **Slow orbit** - Showcase pet from all angles
- **Tilt up/down** - Discover something together with pet

### High Energy (Running, Chasing, Comedy)
- **Fast tracking following** - Match pet's speed
- **Quick pan** - Catch fast action
- **Fast zoom in** - Punchline surprise
- **Handheld with energy** - Bouncy movement matches excitement
- **Low angle running toward camera** - Dramatic heroic moment

### Comedic Timing
- **Static → sudden movement** - Build expectation, then surprise
- **Slow push in on reaction face** - Let the expression land
- **Rapid cuts between angles** - Chaos energy (use sparingly, in editing not per-prompt)
- **Wobble after impact** - Cartoon-style physics

## Pet-Specific Filming Tips

### Filming Cats
- **Eye level or slightly above** - Cats look best when not shot from too low
- **Capture the slow blink** - The "cat kiss" is peak heartwarming content
- **Use vertical space** - Cats on shelves, counters, windowsills create natural framing
- **Patience pays off** - Static camera waiting for cat to do something unexpected
- **Zoom in on toe beans** - Extreme close-up of paw pads is universally loved
- **Capture ear language** - Ears rotating, flattening, perking tell the emotional story

### Filming Dogs
- **Low angle for small dogs** - Makes them look more substantial, adds comedy
- **Tail-level shots** - Wagging tail wagging = instant mood indicator
- **Capture the shake** - Post-bath shake or full-body shake is great slow-mo content
- **Tongue out moments** - Happy dogs with tongues out = maximum likability
- **Fetch/chase sequences** - Natural action sequences dogs perform effortlessly
- **Sleepy head droops** - Fighting sleep is universally funny and cute

### Filming Small Pets (Rabbits, Hamsters, etc.)
- **Macro/close-up heavy** - Small subjects need tight framing
- **Oversized props** - Tiny pet + giant everyday object = automatic scale comedy
- **Top-down for enclosures** - Natural view into their world
- **Fast motion** - Small animals move quickly; match that energy or slow it down for effect

### Filming Multiple Pets
- **Shot-reverse-shot** - Pet A looks at Pet B, then Pet B's reaction
- **Wide for interaction** - Full body language visible
- **Split attention** - Both pets doing something different in same frame

## Common Shot Combinations

### The "Sneak Attack" Sequence (Comedy)
1. **Static wide** - Pet frozen in "hunting" pose
2. **Slow dolly in** - Pet creeping forward, belly low
3. **Close-up** - Intense focused eyes
4. **Fast tracking** - The pounce!
5. **Static medium** - The aftermath (missed, or caught, or surprised by target)

### The "Morning Routine" Sequence (Heartwarming)
1. **Close-up** - Eyes slowly opening from sleep
2. **Medium** - Big stretch (front paws, then back)
3. **Wide** - Walking to food bowl in golden morning light
4. **Overhead close-up** - Eating with contentment sounds
5. **Medium close-up** - Licking lips, satisfied look at camera

### The "Epic Fail" Sequence (Funny)
1. **Low angle wide** - Pet preparing for heroic jump with dramatic lighting
2. **Slow motion medium** - Launch with exaggerated effort
3. **Wide** - Missed the target, clumsy landing
4. **Close-up** - "I meant to do that" recovery expression

### The "Secret Life" Sequence (Narrative)
1. **Wide establishing** - Empty room, owner has left
2. **Medium** - Pet emerges from hiding spot
3. **Tracking shot** - Pet exploring forbidden areas
4. **Close-up** - Pet doing something shocking (typing laptop, answering phone)
5. **Fast zoom** - Sudden sound of door opening
6. **Static wide** - Pet back in innocent position, owner enters none the wiser

## Duration & Pacing Guide

### Pacing Rules
- **First shot ≤ 3 seconds** - Must hook immediately
- **Longest shots in the middle** - Build-up needs time to develop
- **Final shot ≤ 5 seconds** - Clean, memorable exit
- **Vary shot lengths** - Rhythm keeps attention (short-long-short pattern)

### Duration Distribution

For **30-second** videos:
```
Shot 1:  3s  (10%)  - Hook, must be impactful
Shot 2:  5s  (17%)  - Setup
Shot 3:  7s  (23%)  - Build-up (longest, story develops)
Shot 4:  7s  (23%)  - Complication/escalation
Shot 5:  5s  (17%)  - Climax
Shot 6:  3s  (10%)  - Outro/punchline
```

For **60-second** videos:
```
Act 1 (0-15s):  2-3 shots, establishing
Act 2 (15-30s): 2-3 shots, developing
Act 3 (30-45s): 2-3 shots, climax region
Act 4 (45-60s): 1-2 shots, resolution
```

## Sound Design Basics

### BGM Selection by Tone

| Content Type | BGM Style | Timing |
|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Funny prank | Upbeat, playful, cartoonish | Starts at hook, builds to climax |
| Heartwarming | Soft piano, acoustic guitar, gentle strings | Enters at tender moment, swells at emotional peak |
| Epic/fail | Dramatic orchestral → record scratch/silence | Dramatic until fail moment, then comedy sound |
| Daily vlog | Lo-fi hip hop, chill beats | Consistent background throughout |
| Cute overload | Light bells, xylophone, baby-like sounds | Sparingly, for emphasis on cutest moments |
| Suspense/mystery | Low rumble, ticking, minimal | Until reveal, then resolution sound |

### Sound Effect Moments
- **Treat sound as punctuation** - Use for emphasis, not constant background
- **Key SFX moments:** Pounce (whoosh), food landing (plop), door opening (creak), surprised gasp (gasp SFX), happy bark/meow (reaction)
- **Silence is powerful** - Brief moment of no sound before punchline amplifies impact
- **Ambient sound grounds the scene** - Room tone, outdoor sounds make footage feel real
