## Funny/Contrast Templates

### Template F1: "The Sneak Thief"
Pet secretly steals food/treats when owner isn't looking, escalating antics lead to getting caught.

**Structure (30s):**
- **Hook (0-3s):** Pet looking innocent while owner turns away
- **Setup (3-10s):** Owner places food on table, leaves room
- **Build-up (10-20s):** Pet sneaks closer → steals a bite → gets away with it → goes for more
- **Climax (20-25s):** Owner returns unexpectedly, catches pet mid-bite
- **Punchline (25-30s):** Pet freezes with guilty expression, food hanging from mouth

**Key beats:** Innocent look → sneaky movement → escalation → caught red-handed → guilt face

### Template F2: "Expectation vs Reality"
Split-screen or sequential contrast between what the pet thinks it's doing vs what it actually looks like.

**Structure (30s):**
- **Hook (0-3s):** Majestic slow-motion entrance (the "expectation")
- **Setup (3-12s):** Pet attempting something heroic (jumping, catching, looking cool)
- **Build-up (12-22s):** The attempt goes slightly wrong in a cute way
- **Punchline (22-30s):** Reality revealed - messy, clumsy, but adorable failure

**Key beats:** Dramatic setup → heroic attempt → comedic failure → cute aftermath

### Template F3: "The Revenge Plot"
Pet gets pranked/ignored by owner, plans elaborate (but adorable) revenge.

**Structure (30s):**
- **Hook (0-3s):** Owner pranks pet or ignores its request for attention
- **Setup (3-12s):** Pet gives a "you'll regret this" look, starts plotting
- **Build-up (12-22s):** Pet executes revenge plan (hiding toy, sitting on laptop, knocking something over)
- **Punchline (22-30s):** Owner's frustrated reaction vs pet's smug satisfaction

**Key beats:** Wronged → plotting face → revenge execution → smug victory

### Template F4: "The Dramatic Overreactor"
Pet reacts dramatically to minor everyday things.

**Structure (15s):**
- **Hook (0-3s):** Something minor happens (leaf falls, shadow moves)
- **Action (3-10s):** Pet's exaggerated reaction - jumps, fur puffs up, runs in circles
- **Punchline (10-15s):** Realizes it's nothing, tries to play it cool

**Key beats:** Minor trigger → over-the-top reaction → embarrassed recovery

### Template F5: "Caught on Camera"
Secret camera captures what pet does when home alone.

**Structure (30s):**
- **Hook (0-3s):** Owner says goodbye, leaves. Door closes.
- **Setup (3-8s):** Pet waits, listens, confirms owner is gone
- **Build-up (8-22s):** Chaos ensues - jumps on forbidden couch, raids trash, throws a solo party
- **Punchline (22-30s):** Car pulls up. Pet scrambles back to innocent position just in time.

**Key beats:** Goodbye → waiting → secret chaos → emergency reset → innocent face

## Heartwarming Templates

### Template H1: "The Welcome Home"
Emotional reunion when owner returns home.

**Structure (15s):**
- **Hook (0-3s):** Pet's ears perk up, recognizing footsteps/key sound
- **Build (3-10s):** Excitement builds - running to door, tail wagging furiously
- **Resolution (10-15s):** Jumping into owner's arms, happy reunion hug

**Key beats:** Recognition → anticipation → explosive joy → tender reunion

### Template H2: "The Guardian"
Pet shows protective/caring behavior in a quiet moment.

**Structure (30s):**
- **Hook (0-5s):** Quiet scene - baby sleeping, owner feeling sad, or something fragile
- **Setup (5-15s):** Pet notices, approaches slowly and carefully
- **Build-up (15-25s):** Pet gently places paw, snuggles close, or stands guard
- **Resolution (25-30s):** Tender moment of connection - both at peace

**Key beats:** Quiet vulnerability → pet notices → gentle response → wordless comfort

### Template H3: "First Meeting"
Meeting a new friend (another pet, baby, or rescued animal).

**Structure (30s):**
- **Hook (0-5s):** New arrival being introduced
- **Setup (5-15s):** Cautious approach - sniffing, curious observation
- **Build-up (15-25s):** Breaking the ice - first touch, shared toy, or parallel play
- **Resolution (25-30s):** Cuddled together, new friendship formed

**Key beats:** Caution → curiosity → connection → friendship

### Template H4: "The Little Helper"
Pet tries to help owner with daily tasks in adorable ways.

**Structure (30s):**
- **Hook (0-5s):** Owner struggling with something (folding laundry, working, cooking)
- **Setup (5-15s):** Pet watches, decides to "help"
- **Build-up (15-25s):** Chaotic but cute assistance - sitting on papers, carrying one sock
- **Resolution (25-30s):** Owner smiles, gives pet a head pat. Task may be ruined but heart is full.

**Key beats:** Problem → pet's decision to help → chaotic assistance → gratitude over results

### Template H5: "Growing Up Together"
Time-lapse style story of pet and owner bond over time.

**Structure (60s):**
- **Act 1 (0-15s):** First day - tiny puppy/kitten in new home, exploring cautiously
- **Act 2 (15-30s):** Settling in - playtime, first tricks, personality emerging
- **Act 3 (30-45s):** Everyday moments - walks together, cuddles, shared routines
- **Act 4 (45-60s):** Present day - mature pet, deep bond, quiet understanding. Maybe full circle moment.

**Key beats:** New beginnings → discovery → daily rituals → enduring bond

## Story Structure Guide

### 15-Second Format (3-4 shots)
```
Shot 1 (3s):  HOOK - grab attention immediately
Shot 2 (7s):  ACTION - one clear, funny/cute action
Shot 3 (5s):  PAYOFF - the reaction, punchline, or "aww" moment
```

### 30-Second Format (5-7 shots)
```
Shot 1 (3s):   HOOK - scroll-stopping opening image
Shot 2 (5s):   SETUP - establish the situation
Shot 3 (7s):   BUILD-UP - action escalates or develops
Shot 4 (7s):   COMPLICATION - something unexpected happens
Shot 5 (5s):   CLIMAX - peak moment
Shot 6 (3s):   OUTRO - reaction, resolution, or call-to-action
```

### 60-Second Format (7-10 shots)
```
Act 1 (0-15s):  BEGINNING - introduce character + situation + want/need
Act 2 (15-30s): MIDDLE - attempts, complications, escalation
Act 3 (30-45s): CLIMAX - turning point, surprise, emotional peak
Act 4 (45-60s): END - resolution, emotional payoff, memorable final image
```

## Hook Formulas

The first 3 seconds determine if viewers scroll past. Use these hook patterns:

| Hook Type | Pattern | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Surprise face | Extreme close-up of exaggerated expression | Shocked eyes, 2cm from camera |
| Action freeze | Dramatic moment frozen mid-action | Mid-jump, mid-pounce, mid-fall |
| Sound trigger | Pet reacting to something off-screen | Ears rotating toward mysterious sound |
| Cute overload | Visual that's immediately adorable | Tiny puppy in giant bed, head barely visible |
| Mystery setup | Something that makes viewers ask "what's happening?" | Pet staring intently at closed door |
| Relatable situation | Human scenario with pet twist | Pet "working" at a laptop with glasses on |

## Punchline/Ending Types

| Type | Best For | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Reaction shot | Funny content | Owner's face + pet's guilty face |
| Snuggle resolution | Heartwarming content | Curling up together, peaceful |
| Twist reveal | Plot-driven content | "The real thief was the cat all along" |
| Loop ending | 15s clips | Returns to starting position for seamless loop |
| Text punchline | Meme-style content | Caption reveals the joke |
| Emotional freeze | Deeply moving content | Single tear, knowing look, quiet loyalty |

## Template Mixing Guide

Individual templates work well, but combining elements from different templates creates more unique, personalized stories. Use these formulas to mix and match.

### Mixing Formula

```
[Hook from Template A] + [Build-up from Template B] + [Ending from Template C]
```

**Rules for mixing:**
- Keep the pet's established personality consistent throughout
- Ensure tone transitions make sense (abrupt shifts from comedy to tragedy rarely work in 30s)
- One twist maximum per short video - don't overcomplicate

### Recommended Mixes

#### Mix 1: "The Sneaky Guardian" (F1 + H2)
**Combination:** The Sneak Thief (F1) build-up + The Guardian (H2) ending

**Story:** Pet is caught "stealing" something (stealing a sock, sneaking into bed) — but the final reveal shows it was actually bringing the item to a sad/sick owner as comfort.
- **Hook:** Innocent face, classic F1 opening
- **Build-up:** Escalating "theft" behavior
- **Twist ending:** Pet gently places stolen item next to owner → heartwarming resolution

**Best for:** Pets with a mischievous reputation but sweet personality

#### Mix 2: "Epic Fail with Consequences" (F2 + F3)
**Combination:** Expectation vs Reality (F2) structure + The Revenge Plot (F3) motivation

**Story:** Pet attempts something heroic, fails comically (F2) — and the failure accidentally triggers the owner's prank. Pet then plots and executes adorable revenge (F3).
- **Hook:** Dramatic slow-mo entrance
- **Act 1:** Heroic attempt → clumsy fail
- **Act 2:** Owner laughs, pet gets "that look"
- **Act 3:** Pet's revenge — sits on laptop, hides phone
- **Punchline:** Owner's frustrated face vs pet's smug face

**Best for:** Energetic, dramatic pets who always bounce back

#### Mix 3: "Secret Life Reunion" (F5 + H1)
**Combination:** Caught on Camera (F5) middle + Welcome Home (H1) ending

**Story:** Secret camera shows pet's chaotic solo activities (F5) — but instead of scrambling back to innocence when owner returns, the pet explodes with joy (H1). Owner sees the mess but is too touched by the reunion to be mad.
- **Hook:** Owner leaves, door closes
- **Build-up:** Secret chaos montage
- **Climax:** Car pulls up, pet runs to door
- **Resolution:** Joyful reunion, pet "confesses" with guilty-adorable face, owner hugs anyway

**Best for:** Clingy, high-energy pets who love their owners fiercely

#### Mix 4: "The Dramatic Helper" (F4 + H4)
**Combination:** The Dramatic Overreactor (F4) reactions + The Little Helper (H4) motivation

**Story:** Pet overreacts dramatically to a minor household event (F4) — but this overreaction is actually its attempt to "help" with a task (H4). The drama IS the helping.
- **Hook:** Tiny trigger (laundry basket tips over)
- **Build-up:** Maximum drama — running in circles, barking at socks
- **Climax:** Pet "saves" one sock by sitting on it protectively
- **Resolution:** Owner finds pet guarding the "rescued" sock like treasure

**Best for:** Bossy, dramatic pets who think they run the house

### Custom Mixing Tips

When the user wants something "more unique" or "not too template-y":

1. **Ask for a contrast:** "你家[pet name]平时是调皮还是乖巧？我们可以做一个‘外表____但内心____’的反差故事。"
2. **Suggest a genre twist:** "我们可以把[Template X]拍成悬疑风/纪录片风/偶像剧风，你想试试吗？"
3. **Combine human + pet arcs:** Give the owner a mini-storyline too — frustrated, surprised, touched — so it's not just a pet monologue.
4. **Use the pet's real habit:** If the user mentions a specific signature behavior, build the entire story around that one action instead of a generic template.
